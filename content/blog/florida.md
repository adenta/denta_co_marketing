Well, it’s late 2024, around four years since I stopped living in the Bay Area. I feel like the whole dialog in tech around geography has calmed down. A lot of really good engineers *have* moved back to the Bay after looking for greener pastures during COVID, especially with the rise of generative AI. I still haven’t moved back, for a reason I don’t see enough people talking about: the weather.

## Data Analysis

Let’s look at how many hours some cities have above a specific temperature. This data was taken from [OpenMeteo](https://open-meteo.com) and is a reflection of the 90 day period between July and September of 2024.

The average number of hours per day where the temperature exceeded 76°F is as follows:

- **Orlando, Florida**: 20.95 hours/day
- **Los Angeles, California**: 8.37 hours/day
- **San Jose, California**: 6.70 hours/day

The average number of hours per day where the temperature exceeded 80°F for each city is as follows:

- **Orlando, Florida**: 12.34 hours/day
- **Los Angeles, California**: 6.19 hours/day
- **San Jose, California**: 4.18 hours/day

Orlando has twice as many warm hours per day as Los Angeles! If you factor in sleep, you are almost never going to be cold in Florida.

## My Day

I *still* get a lot of questions about why I’m still trying to make Florida a thing. Let’s take a look at my daily routine to demonstrate why I believe California is too cold. I’m going to be talking primarily about **Orlando, Florida** and **San Jose, California** in the rest of this piece. In Florida I have spent most of my time in Orlando due to proximity to family. I am choosing to focus on San Jose in California as a stand in for “Not San Francisco” - vibrant tech scene, but not as chilly as San Francisco. I could’ve focused on a city in Southern California, but in my analysis it doesn’t make a ton of difference (eg: LA is on average, only three degrees warmer than San Jose every day of the year). I’m also just looking at the temperature - in reality the humidity in Florida makes my argument stronger, but I can make my case without needing it. I will be focusing on September 18th, 2024 with [data sourced from OpenMeteo](https://open-meteo.com/en/docs/historical-forecast-api#latitude=28.5383,37.3394&longitude=-81.3792,-121.895&start_date=2024-09-17&temperature_unit=fahrenheit&timezone=auto). I also considered inventing some new metric to measure the weather in a city that was basically just the integral of daily temperature (the area under the curve for how much heat you get throughout the day) but that seemed like overkill.

I set my alarm for 8AM, grab a granola bar and start typing things. At 8AM on September 18th, 2024 in Orlando it was seventy four degrees (Fahrenheit). This is warm enough to put on some shorts, grab my laptop and head outside to my [screened in porch](https://www.lanaiguys.com/blog/why-do-florida-homes-have-lanais). At 8AM PT, San Jose is a chilly fifty eight degrees, too cold to work outside in shorts.

By noon Orlando is up to eighty eight degrees, so I will head inside and grab some lunch, before heading back outside. The whole time I’m sipping on ice water and living my best life. Noon in San Jose is chilling at sixty eight degrees., still too cold to work outside in shorts.

Depending on the day, who I am meeting with, and vibes - I might work inside or outside in the afternoon, it really doesn’t matter. Like if I’m meeting someone new, I usually take the meeting inside as not to appear insane. That said, I am able to comfortably work outside in the shade every month of the year. Dec-Feb is a not every day kind of thing, but there are always a couple of nice days. Comparing that to San Jose, I could really only work outside in the afternoon, and just *barely*. On September 18th, it was above 72 degrees between 1-4 PM.

I feel like I’m successfully making the case that working outside is so much better in Florida than California, and thats not even the best part! The real magic of Florida comes with the warm evenings. I like going for a walk either before or after dinner to relax. This is super easy in Florida, as its still 76 degrees by 10PM, so I can vibe outside whenever I feel like it. In San Jose its already under 70 degrees by 5PM.

So yeah this is why I’m still trying to make Florida a thing 🌴🐊🌞. It’s not perfect. I might not stay here forever. I really do like spending most of my time outdoors.
