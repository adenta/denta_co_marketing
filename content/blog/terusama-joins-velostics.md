*August 26, 2021, 10:15 AM CT*

This week, [InnovationMap covered Velostics' acquisition of Terusama](https://houston.innovationmap.com/velostics-acquires-terusama-gaurav-khandelwal-2654771925.html). That is the outside view of the announcement. I wanted to write the inside view.

I started Terusama because I thought truck scheduling and dock operations were more broken than most people realized. A lot of logistics software talks about visibility at a high level. I wanted to solve a more immediate problem: getting trucks loaded and unloaded with less friction, less waiting, and less chaos.

Over time, that conviction only got stronger. The market did not need more dashboards. It needed software that could actually move work through a facility faster.

That is why this move made sense to me.

Velostics already had strong load automation and check-in capabilities. We had built scheduling technology that fit naturally upstream from that workflow. Putting those pieces together creates a better product than either company could deliver alone. It means customers can see demand earlier, stage loads better, reduce delays, and remove more manual error from the process.

Just as important, this gives the product a bigger runway. Joining Velostics means the ideas behind Terusama can reach larger facilities, deeper enterprise workflows, and more teams that need this problem solved now.

I also made this decision because I trust the team. Gaurav and the Velostics team understand the operational reality of logistics software. They are not building for a slide deck. They are building for warehouses, terminals, and plants that need throughput, reliability, and measurable ROI.

For me, this is not an ending. It is a continuation with more leverage.

I am proud of what we built at Terusama. I am even more excited about what this combination makes possible next.
