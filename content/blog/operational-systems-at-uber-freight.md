# Shipping in a workflow-heavy environment

Time at Uber Freight reinforced something I care about a lot now: ambitious software has to stay legible to the people operating it every day.

In environments like that, small product decisions compound:

* naming affects trust
* unclear state transitions create manual work
* weak handoffs push the real decision somewhere else

## Why it still matters

That experience deepened the connection between product judgment and operational reality. Good systems do not just support the happy path. They make it easier to understand what is happening, what needs attention, and what someone should do next.

That same lens carries into current AI work. Models can create leverage, but the surrounding product has to make the workflow calmer, not more fragile.
